The Wave Equation

Disturb something locally, and the disturbance spreads outward through space. That's what waves are.

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = v^2\nabla^2 u$$

What does it say?

The wave equation describes how disturbances propagate. A displacement at one point affects nearby points, which then affect points farther away, creating a traveling wave.

Curvature creates acceleration. If part of a wave is bent upward, it accelerates downward. If it's bent downward, it accelerates upward.

The equation connects two ideas:

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2\nabla^2 u$$

The left side measures how the displacement accelerates in time. The right side measures how curved the wave is in space.

Variables and symbols

The wave equation contains only a few quantities, but each has a specific physical meaning.

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = v^2\nabla^2 u$$

\(u\) represents the quantity that is waving. Depending on the application, it may describe the displacement of a string, a change in air pressure, or an electric field.

\(t\) is time. The equation tracks how the wave changes as time passes.

\(\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2}\) is the second derivative with respect to time. It measures the acceleration of the wave.

\(\nabla^2 u\), called the Laplacian, measures the curvature of the wave in space. Large curvature produces stronger restoring forces.

\(c\) is the wave speed. It determines how quickly disturbances travel through the medium.

Curvature determines acceleration, while the constant \(c\) determines how fast the wave propagates.

A simple solution

The wave equation has many solutions. One of the most important is a traveling sine wave:

$$u(x,t)=A\sin(kx-\omega t)$$

This function represents a wave moving to the right while maintaining the same shape.

The parameters are:

  • \(A\) — amplitude (maximum displacement)
  • \(k\) — wave number, related to wavelength
  • \(\omega\) — angular frequency
  • \(x\) — position
  • \(t\) — time

Substituting this function into the wave equation shows that it is a valid solution whenever

$$\omega = ck$$

This condition links the wave's frequency, wavelength, and propagation speed. It ensures that the disturbance travels through space at speed \(c\).

A traveling sine wave is one example of a solution. Any combination of such waves is also a solution, which is why the wave equation can model complex wave motion.

Why waves move

Imagine a stretched string. If one section is curved upward, tension pulls it back down. That restoring force creates acceleration, and the disturbance propagates through the string.

The wave equation says that spatial curvature determines how the wave accelerates in time.

Where does this appear?

The wave equation appears everywhere in physics. Different systems produce different kinds of waves, but the mathematics is nearly identical.

Vibrating strings: $$u(x,t)=\text{vertical displacement}$$
Sound waves: $$u(x,t)=\text{air pressure variation}$$
Electromagnetic waves: $$u(x,t)=\text{electric or magnetic field}$$

Key takeaways

  • The wave equation describes propagating disturbances.
  • Spatial curvature creates acceleration.
  • The equation is second order in time, so both position and velocity matter.
  • Waves transport energy and information through space.
  • The same PDE governs strings, sound, light, and many quantum systems.
