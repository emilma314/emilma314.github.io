Time Dilation

A light clock in the train's frame ticks in proper time T₀. An observer on the ground sees the light travel a longer diagonal path — so the tick takes longer by a factor of γ.

The red dot marks the starting position of the light pulse. In the train frame the pulse bounces straight up and back — one tick. In the ground frame the train moves right, so the pulse follows a diagonal path and takes longer. The ground clock is paused when the train clock completes its tick; notice the ground pulse hasn't returned yet.
